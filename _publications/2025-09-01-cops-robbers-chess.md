We study the game of Cops and Robbers on chess graphs — graphs whose vertices are the squares of an n×n chessboard and whose edges connect squares reachable by a given chess piece in one move. We provide a complete classification of the cop numbers for all classical chess pieces. In particular, we prove that two cops are insufficient on queen graphs Q_n for n ≥ 10 by analyzing diagonal coverage limits and constructing a robber escape strategy based on a shortest-diagonal invariant Φ.
