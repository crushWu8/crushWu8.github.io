We investigate pursuit-evasion games on chess graphs and provide a complete classification of cop numbers for both knight graphs and queen graphs on n×n boards, finishing the classification for all standard chess piece graphs. We also address whether cop numbers increase monotonically for queen graphs and introduce royal graphs, which extend chess graphs to handle arbitrary piece movements and better represent realistic movement constraints.
