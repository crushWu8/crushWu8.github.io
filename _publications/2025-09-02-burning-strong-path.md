We examine burning and cooling processes on graphs -- diffusion mechanisms where burned or cooled vertices propagate to neighbors with new sources introduced at discrete intervals. We investigate d-fold strong products of paths, which generalize king graphs and model local contagion spread across multiple dimensions. We convert the challenge into a geometric tiling problem and apply the Euler-Maclaurin formula to establish bounds for burning numbers in path products, achieving sharp results under specific number-theoretic constraints. We also introduce liminal burning, a two-player game exploring controlled contagion spread effectiveness, and define k*, the smallest k such that b_k(G) = b(G).
